life-improver

A framework for designing a deliberate life — seven domains, three hundred practices.

FrameworkProduct DesignWellbeingWeb

Overview

Most self-improvement is a pile of disconnected tips. life-improver treats a whole life as a system — seven domains, each with its own practices, drawn from psychology, philosophy, and contemplative traditions.

300+ practices, each a card — filterable by domain, effort, and tradition.

The problem

Advice rarely fails because it is wrong. It fails because it arrives without a map — no sense of where it fits, what it trades against, or what you are already neglecting. People optimise one corner of life while another quietly collapses.

life-improver starts from the opposite premise: name the whole territory first, then place every practice on it. The seven domains — body, mind, craft, relationships, meaning, resources, and play — become a shared vocabulary you can actually reason with.

The build

The whole thing is a single static site — no backend, no accounts. Practices live in a structured data file; the interface is a fast, filterable index that works offline and loads instantly. The constraint is the point: a tool about a deliberate life shouldn't demand a login to begin.
